About Turtle Terrace Hikkaduwa

We are dedicated about maintaining and safeguarding the magnificent marine life that adorns our waters here at our hatchery, which is tucked away along the lovely shores of Hikkaduwa. Through committed conservation efforts and education about their significance in the environment, it is our duty to safeguard the survival of sea turtles, which are in risk of extinction.
The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery was built with love and care to provide a safe haven for sea turtles at all phases of development. In addition to offering a secure environment for their eggs to naturally hatch, we are dedicated to caring for and treating wounded turtles. By doing this, we significantly contribute to the growth of this magnificent species.

Overexploitation poses significant consequences for turtle populations worldwide. The demand for turtle products, such as meat, eggs, shells, and traditional medicines, drives illegal hunting and harvesting. This unregulated exploitation disrupts the natural balance, leading to population decline and loss of biodiversity. Additionally, the relentless poaching of adult turtles disrupts their reproductive cycle and reduces their ability to maintain healthy populations. The consequences of overexploitation on turtles are far-reaching, threatening their survival and disrupting the ecosystems they inhabit.

Prevention methods:

Preventing the overexploitation of turtles requires a combination of conservation efforts, law enforcement, and community engagement. Implementing and enforcing strict laws and regulations against the trade and consumption of turtle products is essential. Increasing surveillance and patrolling of nesting beaches and vulnerable areas can deter poaching activities. Promoting sustainable livelihood alternatives, such as ecotourism, can provide economic incentives that discourage overexploitation. By implementing these prevention methods, we can mitigate the consequences of overexploitation and ensure the survival of turtle populations for future generations.
